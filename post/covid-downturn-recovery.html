After Crisis: How Are Industries Post-Covid?

By Nur Adhyaksa Hamid

JANUARY 2025

The first case of Covid-19 in Indonesia was reported five years ago, marking a turning point in the country's economic growth trends, as observed from the constant gross regional domestic product. Many sectors experienced declines—some temporary, some sharp—while others have managed to maintain their growth or even thrive.

The air transportation and accommodation services sectors were among the hardest hit by the pandemic. Meanwhile, health industries saw a sharp increase.

Sector by sector

How have industries been performing three years after the first COVID case was reported?

Most sectors affected by COVID-19 have rebounded from the downturn. The broader transportation sector which the most affected at that time has largely returned to pre-pandemic levels, except for air travel. While air travel is trending upward, it has yet to reach its pre-pandemic peak.

Some sector, however, experienced accelerated downturn due to the event. Industries such as rubber products, plastic products, crude petroleum, natural gas, and geothermal energy have seen negative trends

In the sector-by-sector analysis, 2020 serves as the reference year.

An industry is considered relatively unaffected if it did not experience a decline during the pandemic or if its upward trend remained uninterrupted.

If an industry experienced a decline but later regained pre-pandemic levels, it is categorized as recovered and grown.

However, if the industry fails to reach its pre-pandemic peak, it is considered to have not recovered.

If an industry was already on a downward trend before the pandemic, and the decline worsened during and continued after the pandemic, it is categorized as having experienced an accelerated decline.

Data is sourced from: Badan Pusat Statistik (BPS); PDRB Atas Dasar Harga Konstan Menurut Subsektor Lapangan Usaha Tahunan (Seri 2010)
